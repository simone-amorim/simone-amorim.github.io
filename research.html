Research


Software Ecosystem Architectures

Ecosystem architectures have emerged as a new research area promising competitive advantages. Based on these advantages, many companies are opening their architectural boundaries to convert their platforms in an ecosystem. This migration process has been doing without specific models or known patterns. Moreover this evolution is often driven by the business environment, that forces the organization to change its business processes, and, transitively, the information system that supports them. The rapid technological evolution also induces a strong pressure to modify the information system in order to make it apt to support new requirements that the legacy technological platform was unable to meet. My research aimed to study relevant aspects of the evolution process of Software Ecosystem Architectures. The final goal is to develop an approach to improve ecosystem architectures design. The approach intends to provide choices for determining which design decisions may be suitable to implementation mechanism, and how these should be employed in order to improve software quality attributes for ecosystem architectures.
